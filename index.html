Essential Cyber Security

Phishing Scams

Be careful with unexpected emails, messages, or calls. Don't click on strange links or download files from people you don't know. Always double check where it's coming from.

Strong Passwords

Create strong and different passwords for each of your accounts. You can use a password manager to safely store them. Don't use personal details like your name or birthday as passwords.

Two-Factor Authentication

Turn on 2FA (two-factor authentication) for all your accounts. It adds extra protection by asking for a second step, like a code sent to your phone, before you can log in.

Public Wifi Safety

Don't log in to important accounts, like your bank or email, when using public Wi-Fi. If you need to, use a VPN to keep your data safe and protected from hackers.

Backup Data

Always keep a backup of your important files. If your device gets hacked, lost, or damaged, you can still recover your data from the backup.

Use Antivirus Software

Install antivirus software on all your devices and keep it updated. It helps find and remove viruses, malware, and other harmful programs before they can cause trouble.
